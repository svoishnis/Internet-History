    The Internet's Origin Story

    • Why was it Invented?

      • - After World War II, our technology at the time for communicating relied on circuit switching
        - Circuit switching – positive and negative connections in a loop. You need a direct connection.
        - With the advent of nuclear bombs, the circuit switching boards were seen as vulnerable to attack. With one nuclear attack the US could lose all communication.
        - After World War II, General Eisenhower worked towards standardizing research and development in a central hub from the previously divided efforts of the individual government branches.
        - There was a fear that investing solely in the military would not end well and that civilian involvement would be needed. ARPA was created for that purpose.

    • ARPA

      • - ARPA – stands for Advanced Research Projects Agency of the United States Department of Defense
        - ARPA manages research between universities, private companies, and the government.
        - ARPANET was one of the first project or initiative in 1966. This was the solution to the vulnerability of the circuit switching technology to attack.
        - ARPANET stands for the Advanced Research Project Agency Network

    • PARC

      • - PARC is a research group started by the Xerox company
        - They invented everything from mouses, GUIs, and most common office tools in order to leverage the military network for business office use to create the “office of the future"
